## 🧪 About Me

I am a chemical engineer and PhD candidate driven by a deep curiosity about how advanced materials can remediate environmental pollution. My research focuses on developing novel adsorbents and oxidation processes to tackle emerging contaminants in water. I thrive at the intersection of material science and chemical engineering, aiming to create sustainable industrial solutions that make a tangible difference.

My journey began in Algeria, where I earned my master's degree with a strong focus on process engineering. Since then, I've had the opportunity to work in both industrial R&D and academic research – from optimising pharmaceutical production lines to synthesising activated carbon from agricultural waste. Every project reinforces my belief that innovative chemistry can solve pressing environmental problems.

Outside the lab, I'm passionate about education. For three years I volunteered as a physics and math teacher, helping students build confidence and achieve academic success. I also serve as a peer reviewer for research in uranium recovery and wastewater treatment, because I believe that science communication and mentorship are essential parts of being a researcher.

**Tech stack:** UV‑C irradiation, H₂O₂ treatment, COD Analysis, UV‑Vis Spectrophotometry  
**Impact:** Achieved >90% degradation of contaminants at 258 nm and an 18% reduction in Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD), demonstrating the effectiveness of AOPs for environmental remediation.
